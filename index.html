FAQ

How does this bypass throttling?
Carriers detect hotspot traffic by inspecting packet headers (TTL values). FastNet routes your Mac's traffic through a local SOCKS5 proxy on your iPhone via USB. To the carrier, it looks like normal phone traffic — no hotspot flag, no throttling.
Is this legal?
FastNet is a standard SOCKS5 proxy — the same technology used by millions of developers daily. How you use your cellular data is between you and your carrier. We recommend reviewing your carrier's terms of service.
Why does it need a USB cable?
USB provides a direct, private tunnel between your Mac and iPhone that bypasses the carrier's hotspot detection entirely. WiFi hotspot traffic is flagged and inspectable by the carrier — USB is not.
Does it work with all carriers?
Yes. FastNet works with any carrier that throttles hotspot: T-Mobile, Verizon, AT&T, Mint Mobile, Visible, Cricket, and others. If your carrier throttles your hotspot, FastNet will bypass it.
What about Windows / Linux?
Currently Mac only. Windows and Linux support are on the roadmap based on demand.
Do I need to jailbreak my iPhone?
No. FastNet works on stock iOS 16+, no jailbreak needed.
Is my data safe?
Yes. Everything runs locally on your devices. FastNet has no servers, collects no data, and requires no account. Your traffic goes directly from Mac to iPhone to carrier — we never see it.
